PRODUCTION BETTING CRITERIA — TWO-TIER LIVE SYSTEM

We are implementing a two-tier approach to live betting. This is the core strategy for which bets go live and which stay in the experiment pipeline.

TIER 1: LIVE BETTING (placed with real money immediately)
A bet qualifies for live placement if ALL of the following are true:
- Opportunity score >= 68
- The market has FULL data coverage, defined as:
  a) Pinnacle odds are available for this fixture (via OddsPapi)
  b) API-Football data is available with full match stats, lineups, and form data
  c) The league is covered by football-data.org OR has 50+ historical fixtures in our API-Football dataset
- The bet has been validated against Pinnacle closing/current line with positive CLV (see Prompt 3)
- The bet passes all existing risk management checks (Kelly sizing, exposure limits, circuit breakers)
- The market is NOT in quarantine or flagged by circuit breakers

TIER 2: EXPERIMENT PIPELINE (paper traded, building confidence)
All bets that do NOT meet Tier 1 criteria continue through the existing experiment pipeline:
- Experiment → Candidate → Promoted graduation path
- Same statistical gates as before (30+ settled, ROI>3%, CLV>1.5%, p<0.10 etc.)
- When an experiment tag gets Promoted, its bets automatically qualify for Tier 1 IF they also meet the opportunity score threshold
- Experiment and Candidate tier bets are NEVER placed with real money regardless of opportunity score

OPPORTUNITY SCORE ADAPTIVE THRESHOLD:
- Starting threshold: 68
- Store this as a configurable parameter in the database (not hardcoded)
- Implement a weekly review job (run Sunday 5am alongside existing weekly jobs):
  a) Analyse the last 7 days of Tier 1 live bet performance
  b) Calculate rolling 30-day CLV and ROI for Tier 1 bets specifically
  c) If rolling 30-day CLV > 3% AND ROI > 5% AND 50+ Tier 1 bets settled:
     - The model MAY reduce the opportunity score threshold by up to 2 points (minimum floor: 60)
     - Log the reasoning for any adjustment
  d) If rolling 30-day CLV < 1% OR ROI < 0%:
     - Increase the threshold by 3 points (no maximum ceiling)
     - Log the reasoning
  e) If data is insufficient (fewer than 50 settled Tier 1 bets), make no adjustment
  f) Every threshold change must be logged in the promotion_audit_log with full context
  g) The model should factor in: CLV trend (improving or declining?), ROI stability (volatile or consistent?), and market diversity (are we profitable across markets or dependent on one?)
- The goal is NOT to blindly lower the threshold. The goal is to find the optimal threshold that maximises CLV > ROI while allowing appropriate scale. If reducing the threshold would dilute edge, don't reduce it.

DATA RICHNESS CLASSIFICATION:
- Create a data_richness_score for each league-market combination
- Factors: Pinnacle coverage %, API-Football historical depth, football-data.org availability, fixture frequency, odds movement data availability
- A league-market must score above 70% data richness to qualify for Tier 1
- Recalculate data richness weekly
- Log which leagues qualify and which don't
