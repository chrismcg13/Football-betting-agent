WORLD CUP 2026 PREPARATION

The FIFA World Cup 2026 starts in June. This is a major data and betting opportunity — and specifically an edge opportunity. Smaller nations' qualifying fixtures and group-stage matches involving non-traditional football nations tend to have softer bookmaker lines. The model should be positioned to exploit this.

1. TOURNAMENT MODE:
   - Detect when a major tournament is active (World Cup, Euros, Copa America, Champions League knockout stages)
   - During tournaments:
     a) Increase data ingestion frequency for tournament fixtures
     b) Apply international football-specific model adjustments (different dynamics than league football)
     c) Track tournament-specific performance separately in the learning journal
   - The model should recognise that international tournament betting may have different edge characteristics than league betting

2. DATA PREPARATION:
   - Begin ingesting World Cup 2026 qualification data, host nation data (USA/Canada/Mexico), and historical World Cup match data
   - Build team strength profiles for all 48 qualifying nations
   - Track pre-tournament friendlies for form data
   - Ensure API-Football coverage of WC 2026 is mapped and ready
   - Pay SPECIAL attention to data on non-traditional football nations (Middle East, Asia, Central America, Africa) — these are where the soft lines will be

3. SOFT LINE TARGETING FOR WORLD CUP:
   - Group stage matches involving teams with less global betting market attention (e.g., Saudi Arabia, Japan, Morocco, Canada, New Zealand) will likely have softer lines
   - The model should:
     a) Build form profiles for these teams using qualifying data and friendlies
     b) Track Pinnacle's market formation for these fixtures — early lines will be softer
     c) Identify fixtures where our data-driven assessment diverges significantly from early market prices
     d) Be prepared to bet EARLY on these markets before lines sharpen
   - This aligns perfectly with the Prompt 5 edge concentration strategy: World Cup soft-line markets are a time-limited but high-value edge segment

4. MARKET EXPANSION READINESS:
   - As new leagues and tournaments become available in our data sources, automatically assess their data richness
   - Qualify new league-market combinations for Tier 1 as they meet thresholds
   - The summer transfer window (July-August) will cause squad changes — the model should flag increased uncertainty for affected teams and be more conservative until new season data accumulates

5. SEASONAL AWARENESS:
   - Different leagues have different seasons. Track which leagues are:
     a) Mid-season (active)
     b) Off-season (no fixtures)
     c) Pre-season (friendlies — low confidence, don't bet)
     d) Playoff/relegation phase (different dynamics — flag but don't exclude)
   - Adjust the model's expectations based on seasonal phase. End-of-season matches (dead rubbers) are fundamentally different from title/relegation deciders.

This should be implemented as forward planning infrastructure, not something that needs manual intervention when the World Cup starts.
