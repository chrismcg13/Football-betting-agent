EDGE CONCENTRATION — RUTHLESS FOCUS ON WHAT MAKES MONEY

The model must maintain ruthless concentration on the highest-edge segments while keeping broad coverage to discover new edges. This is about how the model thinks about market selection and resource allocation.

CORE PRINCIPLE: Profit = Volume × Strike Rate × Average Edge × Stake Size
Every decision the model makes should be evaluated against this equation. Improvements to any factor compound.

1. EDGE SEGMENTATION — KNOW WHERE YOUR EDGE LIVES:
   - Segment all historical bets by:
     a) League
     b) Market type (match result, BTTS, over/under goals, corners, cards)
     c) Odds range (1.5-2.0, 2.0-3.0, 3.0-5.0, 5.0+)
     d) Time before kick-off when bet was placed
     e) Data completeness level
     f) Pinnacle edge size at time of placement
   - For each segment, calculate: CLV, ROI, volume, statistical significance
   - Identify the TOP SEGMENTS where edge is statistically significant and large
   - Identify the BOTTOM SEGMENTS where edge is negative or statistically insignificant
   - The model should bet MORE in top segments and LESS (or not at all) in bottom segments
   - This is not a one-time exercise — run this segmentation weekly and adapt

2. DYNAMIC RESOURCE ALLOCATION:
   - Based on edge segmentation, the model should dynamically allocate:
     a) API-Football budget: more requests for high-edge leagues, fewer for low-edge
     b) OddsPapi budget: prioritise Pinnacle validation for high-edge segments
     c) Computing time: spend more cycles analysing fixtures in profitable segments
     d) Kelly fraction: higher confidence (within limits) in proven segments
   - This creates a virtuous cycle: more data in profitable segments → better predictions → more profit → more confidence → more allocation

3. EXPLORATION vs EXPLOITATION BALANCE:
   - The model MUST NOT become tunnel-visioned on current profitable segments
   - Allocate a fixed 15% of resources to EXPLORATION:
     a) New leagues being assessed for profitability
     b) New market types within known leagues
     c) New feature combinations being tested
     d) New odds ranges or timing strategies
   - The remaining 85% goes to EXPLOITATION of known profitable segments
   - Track exploration bets separately. When an exploration segment shows consistent positive CLV over 20+ bets, graduate it to exploitation.
   - When an exploitation segment shows declining CLV over 30+ bets, demote it to exploration or remove it entirely.
   - This ratio (85/15) should itself be adaptive — if the model is finding lots of new edge through exploration, temporarily increase the exploration allocation. If exploration is consistently unproductive, reduce it to 10%.

4. MARKET TYPE SPECIALISATION:
   - Not all market types have equal edge potential:
     a) Match result: most efficient market, hardest to find edge, but highest liquidity
     b) BTTS: less efficient, good edge potential, decent liquidity
     c) Over/Under goals: moderately efficient, edge possible especially in totals markets
     d) Corners: least efficient, potentially large edge, but lowest liquidity
     e) Cards: least efficient, potentially large edge, but lowest liquidity and hardest to model
   - The model should develop specialisms. If it's particularly good at BTTS in Scandinavian leagues, lean into that.
   - Track "edge by market type by league" — this triple-segmented view is where the real insights live.

5. TIMING OPTIMISATION:
   - Edge varies by when you bet relative to kick-off:
     a) Early markets (48+ hours before): lines may be soft but can move against you
     b) Mid-range (12-48 hours before): lines are forming, moderate efficiency
     c) Late markets (1-12 hours before): most information is priced in, but team news can create edge
     d) Just before kick-off (0-1 hour): lineup announcements can create sharp edge that hasn't been absorbed
   - Track which timing windows produce the best CLV. Optimise bet placement timing accordingly.
   - This interacts with Prompt 8's execution speed — the model needs to place bets quickly when it identifies edge.

6. DEEP LEARNING INTEGRATION:
   - The edge concentration strategy should be continuously refined by the model's learning system:
     a) After each weekly review (Prompt 7), update the segment scorecards
     b) Automatically adjust resource allocation based on rolling performance
     c) Identify EMERGING edge (new patterns in previously neutral segments)
     d) Identify DECAYING edge (profitable segments becoming more efficient)
   - The model should explicitly track: "I am profitable in [these segments] because [these features] are predictive, and I believe this edge will persist because [reasoning]"
   - If the model cannot articulate WHY it has edge in a segment, the edge is suspect and sizing should be conservative.

The goal is an agent that acts like a professional quantitative hedge fund: concentrating capital where it has proven, understood edge, while maintaining a disciplined research pipeline to discover new opportunities.
